Privacy Policy

Introduction

Welcome to Parthenius AI Studio. This Privacy Policy describes how Parthenius AI Studio (operated by Generative AI Teknoloji Anonim Şirketi) collects, uses, and safeguards your personal data when you use our mobile and web AI products and services ("Service"). We are a global company and are committed to protecting your privacy in compliance with applicable laws (including EU GDPR, UK Data Protection Act, California CCPA, Turkish KVKK, and other relevant regulations). By using our Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Service.

We process personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently, only for the purposes explained below. We do not use your data for any purpose that is incompatible with those purposes. In this Policy, "we" or "us" refers to Generative AI Teknoloji A.Ş. (trading as Parthenius AI Studio), and "you" refers to the user of our Service.

Information We Collect

We collect personal information and other data to provide and improve our Service. The types of information we may collect include:

Identifiers: Information you provide when creating an account or interacting with us, such as your name, email address, username, password, and contact details. We may also collect account profile details or preferences if you choose to provide them.

User Content: Any content you submit or generate through the Service, such as images, videos, text prompts, or other media. For example, if you use our AI Studio to create videos or images, the input content (photos, video clips, etc.) and the AI-generated outputs may be considered user content. Note: Most user-generated content (especially large media files like images or videos) is stored locally on your own device by default – our servers only process it temporarily to facilitate the AI generation and do not permanently store these files. We regularly delete any temporary copies from our servers once processing is complete.

Usage Data: Information about how you access and use the Service. This can include your interactions with the app or website (e.g. features used, buttons clicked, pages viewed), timestamps and logs of usage, preferences, and crash reports. We also collect technical data sent by your device, such as your device type, operating system version, unique device identifiers, IP address, browser type, and other system information. Usage Data may also include broad location information inferred from your IP address or device settings (e.g. city or country), as well as location information if you grant the app permission (for example, if a feature requires GPS-based location, we will only collect it with your consent).

Payment Information: If you make purchases or subscribe to paid features, we (through our payment processor) collect payment information such as your credit/debit card number, expiration date, billing address, and transaction details. Note: Payment transactions are handled by third-party processor Stripe (or another listed payment provider) on our behalf. We do not receive or store your full financial account numbers; we only receive limited information such as a payment confirmation, masked card digits, and subscription status needed for record-keeping.

We may also receive information from third-party sources if you link or integrate other services with Parthenius AI Studio. For instance, if you log in via a third-party platform or use an API integration, that provider may send us certain information (according to their privacy settings and policies).

How We Use Your Information

Provide and Maintain the Service: We use personal data to allow you to register and authenticate your account, and to provide the core features of our AI Studio. For example, we use your information to generate AI content based on your inputs, to save your projects or preferences, and to deliver the functionality you expect. We also use contact details (like your email) to send necessary communications, such as account confirmations, transactional notifications (e.g. purchase receipts), important service announcements, or support responses.

Improve and Develop the Service: We process usage data and feedback to understand how our Service is used and to make improvements. This includes fixing bugs, monitoring performance, optimizing algorithms, and developing new features. For instance, we may analyze aggregate usage patterns to improve user interface design or to ensure the AI models are performing efficiently. We may also use crash logs or error reports to troubleshoot and enhance stability.

Personalization: To the extent permitted, we may use certain data to personalize your experience. For example, remembering your preferences, interface settings, or past content can help tailor the Service to you. Any such profiling or personalization will be done in line with applicable laws and, where required, with your consent.

Payments and Transactions: We use payment and contact information to process your purchases or subscription fees. This includes charging your payment method through our payment processor (e.g. Stripe) and keeping a record of your transactions. We may also use this info to detect and prevent fraudulent payments or abuse of our payment system.

Communicate with You: We use your contact information (such as email) to send service-related communications. These include verification emails, password reset instructions, subscription or billing notices, updates to our policies, or customer support replies. If you opt in to receive marketing communications or newsletters, we will use your email to send you promotional content about new features or offers – and you can opt out at any time. We will not spam you, and will send marketing messages only in accordance with your preferences and applicable law.

Security and Abuse Prevention: We process certain data (like IP address, device info, and usage logs) to monitor for suspicious or malicious activity and to enforce our Terms of Service. This helps us to protect the integrity of our platform, investigate potential fraud, hacking or abuse, and ensure users are not violating usage policies. For example, we may use automated tools to flag unusual usage patterns that could indicate bots or misuse, and we may log account actions in case we need to audit or investigate improper behavior.

Legal Compliance: Where necessary, we use and retain your information to comply with legal obligations applicable to us. For instance, we may keep transaction records for tax and accounting purposes, or we may process personal data to comply with lawful requests by public authorities. If we are under a legal duty to disclose data (e.g. a court order or subpoena), or to enforce our agreements or protect our rights, we will use and share data as needed (see Data Sharing below).

Other Purposes with Consent: If we intend to use your data for any purpose not outlined in this Policy, we will explain it to you at the time of collection or request your consent. We will not use sensitive personal data for purposes you have not agreed to.

We limit our use of personal data to what is relevant and necessary for each purpose. We do not use your personal data for automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you without human review (for example, no fully automated account termination without oversight).

Parthenius AI Studio relies on a number of trusted third-party service providers to operate our Service. These third parties process data on our behalf for specific business purposes, such as hosting, analytics, payment processing, and AI computing. We never sell your personal data to third parties. We only share data with these providers to the extent necessary to deliver our Service, under strict obligations of confidentiality and security. The key third-party services we use (and the data they handle) include:

Analytics data collected via Firebase is generally aggregated and does not directly identify you. Therefore, any personal data that is stored or processed by our backend (such as account information, text prompts, or temporary caches of AI generation data) may pass through Google Cloud servers. Google Cloud acts as our data processor, storing data securely and allowing us to scale our Service globally.

Stripe: We integrate Stripe, Inc. as our payment processor for handling subscription fees or other payments. When you enter your payment details, that information is sent directly to Stripe via secure TLS encryption; we do not see or store your full credit card number or CVV code. Stripe processes your payment and then provides us with a confirmation and limited information (such as the last four digits of your card, card type, expiration, and a payment token). Stripe is a PCI-DSS compliant service. Please review Stripe's privacy policy for details on their data handling. We have a contract with Stripe ensuring that they protect your data and only use it for payment processing on our behalf.

OpenAI: Our AI Studio may utilize language or image generation models from OpenAI (e.g., GPT APIs or DALL·E) to provide some of the AI-generated content features. If you enter text prompts or other content to be processed by an OpenAI model, those inputs may be sent to OpenAI's servers and the AI-generated output is returned to our app. We do not use OpenAI's services to process any personal data beyond what you input for generation, and we do not store your prompts or outputs on our servers longer than necessary. (OpenAI's API policies state that they do not use API data to improve their models by default, and they have their own data retention rules.) Nonetheless, your use of such features is subject to OpenAI's terms and privacy policy as well, and by using them you consent to OpenAI processing your data for the purpose of providing the AI output. We will clearly indicate when an AI feature is powered by a third-party model like OpenAI.

Fal.ai and Eachlabs: We partner with Fal.ai (a generative AI model hosting platform) and Eachlabs (AI workflow provider) to run certain AI models (for image, video, or other media generation) more efficiently. When you request an AI generation that uses one of these providers, your input data (such as an image or text prompt) is securely transmitted to their servers which run the model and return the result. We do not permanently store your input or the resulting output on our own servers, but these third-party AI providers may momentarily cache data for processing or performance. We contractually require that any data sent to these AI providers is used only to fulfill your requested generation and not retained longer than necessary or used for other purposes. However, as with OpenAI, use of these services means your data will be processed by them under their privacy practices. We recommend reviewing Fal.ai's and Eachlabs' privacy policies for more information.

Other Service Providers: We may use additional third parties for specific functionalities: for example, email delivery services (to send you verification or support emails), customer support platforms (to manage support tickets or chats), or analytics and tracking tools (such as Google Analytics or similar, if integrated into our website). Any such providers will be limited to accessing the data needed to perform their tasks (e.g. your email address for an email service, or anonymized usage data for analytics). Where required, we will ask your consent for certain analytics or marketing cookies.

We ensure all third-party processors we engage are bound by contractual agreements to protect your data and to use it only for the purposes we specify. They must implement industry-standard security measures and comply with applicable privacy laws. We remain responsible for the handling of your personal data by all such third parties acting on our behalf. Aside from these processors, we will not share your personal data with any other third parties for their own marketing or other independent use without your consent.

Data Storage and Retention

Storage Location: We are a global service; however, the primary data storage for our Service is currently on cloud servers located in the United States and/or European Union (via Google Cloud), depending on where you are located. We strive to store data in the region closest to our users when feasible. Note that personal data may be transferred to or accessible from our headquarters in Turkey for administrative purposes, and our third-party processors (Stripe, OpenAI, etc.) may be located in the U.S. or other jurisdictions. Regardless of location, we apply the same security standards to your data (see Security below) and, where applicable, put legal safeguards in place for international data transfers (see International Data Transfers below).

Local Device Storage: As mentioned, most user-generated content (especially large media like photos and videos) is stored locally on your device by design. Our mobile app keeps your projects, generated media, and edits on your phone/tablet unless you actively choose to back them up or share them. This means that, in normal usage, we do not have a copy of your creative content on our servers. Temporary data may pass through our servers during AI processing (for example, an image might be uploaded temporarily to generate a result), but we do not retain that data longer than necessary to complete the operation. We periodically clear cache and temporary storage on our servers to ensure that user content isn't stored beyond its purpose. In short, we do not permanently store your images, videos, or chat prompts on our servers – those remain under your control on your device. (The only exceptions would be if you explicitly use a cloud backup/sync feature that we offer, in which case we will inform you and get consent, or if a piece of content triggers a moderation process due to violating policies, which might lead us to retain it for investigation.)

Retention Period: We retain personal data only as long as needed to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy, or as required by law. The retention period can vary by data type and context:

  • Account Information: We keep your account registration info (like name, email, and login credentials) for as long as your account is active. If you decide to delete your account or you have not used the Service for an extended period, we will initiate deletion of your account data from our live systems. In general, once you delete your account, we will remove or anonymize personal data within approximately 30 days, barring unforeseen technical delays. Some residual data (e.g. in backups) may take slightly longer to purge, but we will not restore or use it except for security, fraud prevention, or legal obligations.
  • User Content and Generated Output: Any user content that remains only on your device is under your control – you can delete it at any time from the app or your device storage. For content that passed through our servers for processing, we generally do not store it after the process completes. If any such data is temporarily cached, it is typically deleted within hours. AI-generated outputs are not stored on our servers beyond delivering them to you, unless they are associated with your account (e.g., a small metadata record that a generation occurred). We do not build user-specific profiles of your content usage.
  • Transaction Data: We retain payment transaction records, subscription status, and purchase history as long as you use the Service and for a reasonable period thereafter (to maintain financial records for accounting/audit purposes). Even after account deletion, we may retain invoices or minimal transaction logs as required by finance and tax regulations. However, as noted, we never store raw payment card details ourselves.
  • Usage Logs and Analytics: Low-level usage logs (like server request logs, IP addresses, device events) are typically kept for a short period (a few weeks to a few months) for debugging and security monitoring, then either deleted or aggregated. Aggregated analytics data (which no longer identifies individuals) may be retained longer for historical analysis.
  • Communications: If you contacted support or we corresponded via email, those communications may be retained for a period to ensure we have context for any follow-up. Typically, support emails and chat logs are kept for a few years at most, unless needed longer for legal reasons (e.g., evidence in a dispute).
  • Legal Requirements and Preventing Harm: We may need to retain certain information if necessary to meet legal obligations or resolve disputes. For example, if we deactivate an account due to abuse, we might retain a record of that user's email or IP to block re-registration. Or if a law enforcement request or preservation order is in effect, we will retain the relevant data as required. We will also retain data as necessary to comply with any record-keeping obligations (such as financial records for X years under tax law).

When we no longer have a legitimate need to retain your personal information, we will securely delete it or anonymize it. If deletion is not immediately possible (for instance, if the data is in backups), we will isolate it from further use until deletion is feasible.

By using the Service or providing us your information, you consent to the transfer of your personal data to these countries, subject to the safeguards described above. Your Rights and Choices

You have certain rights regarding your personal data, which you can exercise under applicable data protection laws. We are committed to honoring your rights. These rights include, as applicable:

Right to Access: You can request confirmation of whether we are processing your personal data, and if so, request a copy of the data we hold about you. This allows you to know and verify the lawfulness of our processing.

Right to Rectification: If any of your information is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct or update it. We encourage you to keep your profile information current, and you may do so directly in the app settings (for basic account info) or by contacting support for assistance.

Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): You may ask us to delete your personal data under certain circumstances. For example, if you no longer want to use the Service, you can request account deletion, and we will remove personal data that we are not required to keep. This right is not absolute – if we have a legal obligation or compelling legitimate interest to retain certain data (for instance, to comply with a regulatory requirement or if the data is relevant to an open legal claim), we may decline the request for those specific pieces of data but will explain why.

Right to Data Portability: You have the right to request a copy of the personal data you provided to us in a machine-readable format, and you can ask that we transmit it to another service provider if technically feasible. For example, you may request an export of your account information and any content you've provided, so you can reuse it elsewhere.

Right to Restrict Processing: You can request that we temporarily limit our processing of your data in certain situations – for instance, while you contest the accuracy of the data or object to our processing, or if we no longer need the data but you want us to preserve it for legal reasons. When processing is restricted, we will store your data securely and not use it (except as needed for legal claims, with your consent, or to protect others) until the restriction is lifted.

Right to Object: You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data when that processing is based on legitimate interests or performed for direct marketing purposes. If you object, we will cease the processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, or if it's needed for legal reasons. For direct marketing, if you object or opt-out, we will stop sending you marketing communications.

Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent to process personal data (for example, for sending promotional emails or using non-essential cookies), you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. You can do so by changing your settings in the app (if available), clicking "unsubscribe" in an email, or contacting us. Note that withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of any processing done before your withdrawal. If you withdraw consent for a certain feature, that feature might not be available to you (for instance, if you disable location permission, location-based features will be limited).

Additional Rights (California and Other Jurisdictions): If you are a resident of certain states or countries, you may have additional rights. For example, California residents have the right to request a notice disclosing the categories of personal information and specific pieces of information we have collected about you, as well as the categories of sources, purposes for collecting, and third parties with whom we share that information (essentially, the contents of this Privacy Policy). You also have the right to request that we delete personal information (subject to similar exceptions as above), and the right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information if applicable. As stated, we do not sell personal data to third parties for monetary consideration. We also do not share personal data for cross-context behavioral advertising in a way that would be considered a "sale" or "share" under CCPA. If that ever changes, we will provide a "Do Not Sell or Share" link.

California law also provides that you have a right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your privacy rights. We honor that: if you exercise any of your rights, we will not deny you goods or services, charge you a different price, or provide a lesser quality of service because of your choices.

Turkish residents: You have rights under KVKK (Turkey's data protection law) including the right to learn if we hold your data, to request correction or deletion of inaccurate or unlawfully processed data, and to object to certain processing. You may contact us as described below to exercise these rights.

Other regions: If other privacy laws apply to you (such as Brazil's LGPD, Canada's PIPEDA, etc.), we will gladly facilitate your rights requests in line with those laws as well.

Exercising Your Rights: You can submit requests to access, correct, delete, or exercise any of your data rights by contacting us (see Contact Us section below). For certain rights (like data download or deletion), we may also provide in-app options or self-service tools. For example, you may delete your account from within the app's account settings (if available), which triggers our deletion process. When you contact us with a rights request, we will need to verify your identity to protect your privacy – this may involve confirming control of your email address or providing identifying information. We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by law (for instance, within 30 days for GDPR requests, which may be extended if necessary with notice to you). If we decline your request, we will explain the reason (e.g., if it's manifestly unfounded or excessive, or if a legal exception applies).

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (for example, in the EU you can contact your country's data protection authority; in the UK the ICO; in Turkey the KVKK board; in California the Attorney General or CPPA). We would, however, appreciate the chance to address your concerns first, so we encourage you to contact us with any complaint and we will do our best to resolve it.

Security Measures

We take the security of your personal data very seriously and implement industry-standard security measures to protect it from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security program includes both technical and organizational safeguards:

Encryption: All network communication between your app/browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS. This means that data in transit is protected from eavesdropping. For sensitive data at rest (such as passwords, which are stored as cryptographic hashes), we apply strong encryption or hashing.

Access Control: We limit access to personal data strictly to those employees, contractors, and service providers who need it to operate or improve the Service. Access is controlled through role-based permissions, and our personnel are required to adhere to confidentiality obligations. Administrative access to systems is secured with multi-factor authentication and logging.

Secure Development Practices: Our engineering team follows secure coding guidelines and we regularly update our software dependencies to address security vulnerabilities. We also employ protections against common web attacks (like SQL injection, XSS, CSRF) in our platform.

Network and Infrastructure Security: Our servers are protected by firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and continuous monitoring. We deploy updates and patches promptly. We also isolate different systems to minimize the impact of any potential breach (segmentation).

Testing and Auditing: We periodically test our systems for security weaknesses, through practices like penetration testing and code reviews. We maintain logs of system activity and access to data, which helps in detecting and investigating any irregular access. Additionally, when feasible, we enable security features provided by our cloud platform (Google Cloud) such as threat detection.

Third-Party Security: When we engage third-party processors (Firebase, Stripe, etc.), we review their security standards to ensure they meet our requirements. We sign Data Processing Agreements that obligate them to protect your data and notify us of any incidents. We also take steps such as enabling encryption and configuring access keys carefully when using their services.

Organizational Policies: All our staff are trained on data privacy and security. We have internal policies in place for how to handle user data, and we limit personal device access to production data. Team members with access to personal data undergo additional vetting and are required to use secure devices and connections.

Despite our efforts, no system can be 100% secure. We therefore cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. However, we will continue to update and improve our security measures to combat new threats. In the unlikely event of a data breach that affects your personal data, we will promptly notify you and the relevant authorities as required by law. We also have an incident response plan in place to handle such situations effectively.

You also play a role in keeping your data safe. Please use a strong, unique password for your account and do not share it. Notify us immediately if you suspect any unauthorized access to your account. Be mindful of phishing attempts – we will never ask for your password via email. If you use our Service on a public or shared device, make sure to log out when you're done.
